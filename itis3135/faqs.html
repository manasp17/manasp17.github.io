Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is BMR? BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to perform basic functions at rest, such as breathing, digestion, and circulation.
  • Why is BMR important? BMR helps estimate how many calories you need to consume each day to maintain your current weight. It is a useful tool for weight management and understanding your caloric needs.
  • How do I calculate my BMR? You can calculate your BMR using personal information such as age, gender, weight, and height. This can be done using our BMR calculator on the website.
  • What factors affect BMR? Factors that can affect your BMR include age, gender, body composition, and genetics.
  • What is TDEE? TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories you burn in a day, considering your BMR and physical activity level.
  • How can I increase my BMR? Increasing physical activity, building muscle mass, and eating a balanced diet can help boost your BMR.
  • What is the difference between BMR and TDEE? BMR measures the calories needed for basic body functions at rest, while TDEE accounts for calories burned during physical activity and daily movements.
  • Can my BMR change over time? Yes, BMR can change due to factors such as age, weight loss or gain, muscle mass changes, and metabolic disorders.
